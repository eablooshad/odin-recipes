Odin recipes: Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup

Pounded yam is a smooth dough-like meal made from yam flour and usually served with Egusi soup. Egusi soup is made from melon seeds and typically includes leafy vegetables, palm oil, meat (like goat, beef, or fish), and seasonings.

Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup is a popular and traditional Nigerian dish. Pounded Yam is a smooth, dough-like staple made from yam, and Egusi Soup is a rich, flavorful soup made with melon seeds. Here's how to make both:

Pounded Yam

Ingredients

  • Yam Tubers (Required)
  • Water

Procedure

  1. Peel and Cut the Yam:
    • Peel the yam tubers and cut them into medium-sized chunks.
  2. Boil the Yam:
    • Place the yam chunks in a pot and cover them with water. Boil until they are very soft, which usually takes about 15 to 20 minutes.
  3. Pound the Yam:
    • Traditionally, the yam is pounded with a mortar and pestle until smooth and stretchy. However, you can also use a food processor or stand mixer if a mortar and pestle are not available.
    • Once pounded, the yam becomes smooth and stretchy, forming a dough-like consistency.
  4. Serve:
    • Mold the pounded yam into balls or shapes of your choice and serve immediately with Egusi Soup.

Egusi Soup

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of ground Egusi (melon seeds)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups of stock (chicken, beef, or fish)
  • Assorted meats (such as beef, goat meat, chicken, or fish)
  • 1 cup of spinach or bitter leaf, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2 Scotch bonnet peppers, blended (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and seasoning cubes (to taste)

Procedure

  1. Prepare the Egusi:
    • In a bowl, mix the ground Egusi with a little stock or water to form a thick paste.
  2. Cook the Meat:
    • In a separate pot, cook the assorted meats with a bit of onion, salt, and seasoning until tender. Set aside.
  3. Make the Egusi Balls:
    • Heat half of the palm oil in a pot and add the Egusi paste in lumps. Cook until they begin to float to the top, about 5 to 10 minutes. Remove the Egusi balls and set aside.
  4. Make the Soup Base:
    • In the same pot, add the remaining palm oil, onions, and garlic. Saut√© until the onions are translucent.
    • Add the cooked meat, blended Scotch bonnet peppers, and remaining stock. Bring to a boil.
  5. Add the Egusi:
    • Return the cooked Egusi balls to the pot. Add the ground crayfish. Simmer on low heat for about 15 minutes.
  6. Add Greens:
    • Add the chopped spinach or bitter leaf. Cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  7. Season:
    • Adjust the seasoning with salt and additional seasoning cubes if needed.
  8. Serve:
    • Serve the Egusi Soup hot with the Pounded Yam.

Enjoy your Pounded Yam and Egusi Soup! This hearty and satisfying meal is a staple in Nigerian cuisine and is perfect for special occasions or a family dinner.
