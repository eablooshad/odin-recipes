Odin Recipes: Moi Moi

Moi Moi, also known as Moin Moin, is a traditional Nigerian steamed bean pudding made primarily from ground peeled beans. It is a versatile and nutritious dish often flavored with various ingredients like fish, eggs, or vegetables. Here's how to prepare it:

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of black-eyed peas or brown beans
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1-2 red bell peppers, chopped
  • 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 2-3 tablespoons of ground crayfish (optional)
  • 1-2 teaspoons of ground nutmeg
  • Salt and seasoning cubes to taste
  • Optional: cooked and flaked fish, diced cooked liver, hard-boiled eggs, or diced vegetables

Procedure

  1. Bean Preparation:
    • Soak the beans in cold water for a few hours or overnight to soften them. This helps in peeling off the skins.
    • Rub the beans between your hands or in a blender to remove the skins, then rinse repeatedly to separate the skins from the beans.
  2. Blend the Ingredients:
    • In a blender, combine the peeled beans, onions, bell peppers, and Scotch bonnet peppers. Blend to a smooth, thick paste, adding a little water if necessary.
  3. Mix the Bean Batter:
    • Pour the blended mixture into a large bowl. Add the vegetable oil, ground crayfish, nutmeg, salt, and seasoning cubes. Mix thoroughly to ensure everything is well combined.
    • If adding fish, liver, eggs, or vegetables, gently fold them into the mixture at this stage.
  4. Prepare for Steaming:
    • Traditionally, Moi Moi is steamed in banana leaves, which imparts a unique flavor. However, you can also use ramekins, aluminum cups, or specially made Moi Moi plastic containers.
    • Lightly oil the containers or leaves, then pour the bean mixture into them, filling only about three-quarters full to allow room for expansion.
  5. Cook the Moi Moi:
    • Place the filled containers in a large pot. Add water to the pot, about halfway up the sides of the containers.
    • Cover the pot and allow the Moi Moi to steam on medium to low heat. Cooking time varies depending on the size of your containers but typically ranges from 45 minutes to 1 hour. Check periodically to ensure there's still water in the pot, adding more if needed.
  6. Check for Doneness:
    • Moi Moi is done when it solidifies and a toothpick inserted into the middle comes out clean.
  7. Serve:
    • Allow the Moi Moi to cool slightly before unwrapping or removing from the containers.
    • Serve as a main dish or as an accompaniment to rice, pap (ogi), or bread.

Enjoy your Moi Moi! It's a flavorful and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed at any time of the day.
